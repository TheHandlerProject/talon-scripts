## Rebound Orthopedics Data Breach -- $2.5 Million Settlement

### What Happened

On **February 1, 2024**, an unauthorized actor gained access to Rebound Orthopedics & Neurosurgery's computer systems. The breach continued until **February 3, 2024**, when suspicious network activity was detected. During that window, files containing **protected health information (PHI) of 426,536 patients** were accessed and potentially copied.

### Data Exposed

The following categories of patient data were compromised:

- Full names
- Dates of birth
- **Social Security numbers**
- **Driver's license numbers**
- **Passport numbers**
- Medical information
- Health insurance information
- **Financial account information**

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| February 1, 2024 | Unauthorized access to Rebound systems begins |
| February 3, 2024 | Suspicious activity detected; breach discovered |
| February 10, 2024 | Internal investigation concludes |
| April 15, 2024 | Rebound begins notifying affected patients |
| February 5-7, 2025 | First class action lawsuits filed in Clark County Superior Court |
| December 19, 2025 | Preliminary court approval of settlement |
| May 28, 2026 | Deadline for claims, objections, and exclusions |
| June 12, 2026 | Final settlement approval by Judge Gregory Gonzales |

### Key Allegations

The consolidated lawsuit (*Cooper, et al. v. Rebound Orthopedics & Neurosurgery P.C.*) alleged:

- Rebound **failed to implement reasonable and appropriate cybersecurity measures**
- Rebound **did not notify patients for over a year** after the breach
- Rebound **failed to disclose whether the breach was contained or ended**
- Rebound **did not identify how the breach occurred**

Legal claims included:
- Negligence
- Breach of implied contract
- Unjust enrichment
- Breach of fiduciary duty
- Invasion of privacy
- Violations of the **Washington Consumer Protection Act**
- Violations of the **Oregon Unlawful Trade Practices Act**

### Settlement Terms

- **Total fund:** $2,500,000
- **Option A:** Up to $5,000 for documented out-of-pocket losses (identity theft, credit monitoring fees, ID replacement costs) with third-party proof
- **Option B:** Approximately $75 one-time pro rata cash payment
- **Credit monitoring:** Two years of CyEx Medical Shield Complete (identity theft insurance, victim assistance, credit monitoring)
- **Claims:** ReboundDataSettlement.com

**Rebound denied all fault and liability** despite paying $2.5 million.

### Practice Scale

Rebound Orthopedics & Neurosurgery operates **11 locations** across Washington, employs **40+ staff**, and generates approximately **$10 million in annual revenue**.
