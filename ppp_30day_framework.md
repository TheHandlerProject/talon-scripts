# Proper Paws Project — 30-Day Curriculum Framework
> Internal development reference. Pull from this to build daily lesson modules in the PPP app.

---

## How This Is Structured

Each day has:
- **Phase** — which week/block this belongs to
- **Objective** — what the handler is building today
- **Task** — what they actually do
- **Gate** — the pass/fail test before advancing
- **Game** — the standalone training game for that day

Gates are hard stops. The app enforces them. No day unlocks until the gate is passed.

---

## Phase 1 — Restoring Food Drive (Days 1–10)

---

### Day 1
**Title:** Why Your Dog Doesn't Work for Food  
**Objective:** Handler mindset reset. Understand why food drive is the foundation of everything.  
**Task:** Reading day. No physical protocol. Have the household conversation tonight. Decide honestly whether you can hold the line.  
**Gate:** Internal. Can you let your dog miss a meal without caving? Yes = proceed. No = don't start yet.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 0: Awareness**
> Hold a piece of kibble flat in your open palm. Don't move toward your dog. Just let them notice it's there. That's it. No feeding, no clicking. This isn't a training session — it's a baseline. How long does it take your dog to notice the food? Note the time. This is your starting point.

---

### Day 2
**Title:** Pull All Free Food. One Meal a Day Starts Now.  
**Objective:** Establish food scarcity. Remove all background access to calories.  
**Task:** Calculate daily ration. Put it down once, 10-minute window, pick it up regardless. Pull every free food source in the house.  
**Gate:** One meal offered with 10-minute timer. All free food sources removed. Yes to both = proceed.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 1: Contact**
> Same as Day 1 — open palm, waist height, wait. This time, when your dog approaches and takes the food, that's a rep. Run as many reps as your dog will take during today's feeding window. No talking. No luring. Just hold it and let them come to you. Count how many reps you get.

---

### Day 3
**Title:** The First Refusal  
**Objective:** Hold the line through the first test refusal. Don't negotiate.  
**Task:** Same protocol as Day 2. Same time, same 10-minute window. Pick up whatever is in the bowl at minute 10. If refused: 24-hour reset, no exceptions.  
**Gate:** Bowl down at same time as yesterday, left 10 minutes, picked up regardless of outcome. Yes = proceed.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 1: Contact (Repeat)**
> Same drill as yesterday. Open palm, waist height, let them come to you. Your only job is to track: are they coming faster today than yesterday? Are they more confident taking the food? Record your observations. No pressure, no shaping — this is pure drive monitoring.

---

### Day 4
**Title:** Holding the Line  
**Objective:** Outlast the dog's willpower test. Audit the environment for access points.  
**Task:** Same bowl protocol. Additionally: walk every room and close every accidental food access point — garbage, counters, cat food, kids' snacks. Confirm with every household member.  
**Gate:** Same time, 10-minute window, no extra food given, no household slippage. Yes = proceed. Any slippage = reset to Day 2.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 2: Patience**
> Open palm, waist height. This time, after your dog takes a piece, wait 3 full seconds before presenting the next one. Build a rhythm: present → dog takes → 3 count → present again. You're introducing mild patience without any formal command. Track total reps. Goal: 15+ clean reps in one session.

---

### Day 5
**Title:** The Bowl-Eating Checkpoint  
**Objective:** Confirm food drive is restored before advancing. Four-criteria test.  
**Task:** Same bowl protocol. Before advancing, dog must meet all four criteria: (1) approaches the bowl immediately, (2) eats without hesitation, (3) finishes within a few minutes, (4) has done this 4–5 consecutive days.  
**Gate:** All four criteria met = advance to Day 6. Any criteria missed = repeat Day 5. No exceptions.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 2: Patience (Consolidate)**
> Same as yesterday. Present → take → 3-second pause → present. This time, during the 3-second wait, watch your dog's eyes. Are they watching your face or watching your hand? Note which. Eye contact on the hand is normal here. We'll use that observation later in Week 2.

---

### Day 6
**Title:** The First Hand-Feeding Session  
**Objective:** Transfer food source from bowl to hand. Handler becomes the paycheck.  
**Task:** Bowl goes away. Sit in a chair, quiet room, dog on a loose leash underfoot. Ration in a bowl on the table next to you. Hold pieces palm-up one at a time and let the dog come take them. Full ration delivered this way. 10-minute rule still applies if dog refuses.  
**Gate:** Dog took food from hand for at least half the session. Eagerly = advance. Reluctantly = repeat. Refused = bowl up, 24-hour reset.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 3: Approach**
> Same chair setup. This time, after a few warm-up reps, hold the kibble out and let the dog approach from across the room. You want them walking toward you to get the food — not you reaching to them. Build the approach habit: food is over here, come get it. Run 10–15 reps. This is the precursor to recall drive.

---

### Day 7
**Title:** Hand-Feeding Consolidation  
**Objective:** Lock in the hand-feeding pattern with consistent engagement.  
**Task:** Same hand-feeding session. Same setup. Build rep count and attention. Look for the dog orienting back to you after each piece.  
**Gate:** Dog worked through session with consistent engagement — taking pieces, eating, returning for more. Yes = on track. Crashed early or refused mid-session = 24-hour reset, repeat tomorrow.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 3: Approach (Repeat)**
> Same as Day 6 game. Track whether the dog's approach speed has increased. Are they trotting toward you instead of walking? That's a green flag. If they're still cautious, no problem — just note it and keep going. Enthusiasm builds with hunger and consistency.

---

### Day 8
**Title:** Building Hunger, Not Starving  
**Objective:** Understand the difference between productive hunger and deprivation. Calibrate ration.  
**Task:** Hand-feeding session as usual. Assess dog's body condition: visible waist tuck, ribs palpable but not visible = healthy and on track. Adjust ration up or down by small increments only if genuinely needed.  
**Gate:** Session ran cleanly. Body condition assessed. Any needed ration adjustment made. Yes to all = advance to Day 9.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 4: Two-Hand Switch**
> Hold food in both hands. Present one hand. When dog takes from it, immediately offer the other hand. Alternate back and forth. You're building the habit of engagement following the handler, not fixating on one source. Run 15–20 reps. Watch for the dog's eyes starting to come up to your face between hands.

---

### Day 9
**Title:** The Trainable-Dog Test  
**Objective:** Diagnostic gate. Three tests confirm the dog is ready for marker work.  
**Task:** Run three tests in sequence:  
1. Hand-feeding in the training space — does the dog take food immediately and consistently?  
2. Mild environmental distraction — does the dog re-orient to you quickly?  
3. Food in your closed fist — does the dog work to get it out vs. give up?  
**Gate:** Strong pass all three = advance. Conditional pass (fail on one) = advance with awareness. Fail two or three = return to hand-feeding consolidation.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 5: Closed Fist**
> Make a fist around a piece of kibble. Present your fist to the dog. Let them sniff, paw, lick. The moment they pull back or pause — even briefly — open your hand and let them take it. You're rewarding the disengage, not the persistence. This is the foundation of the engagement drill in Week 2. Run 10 reps.

---

### Day 10
**Title:** Week 1 Checkpoint  
**Objective:** Confirm all Week 1 foundations before entering marker work.  
**Task:** Five-question self-assessment:
1. Dog eats immediately from your hand every session?
2. Dog finishes the full ration with engagement?
3. Dog re-orients to you after each piece?
4. Dog passed all three trainable-dog tests?
5. You have held the line on all free food for 7+ days?  
**Gate:** All five yes = advance to Day 11. Any no = identify the gap, hold here 3–5 more days, recheck.  
**Game:** 🤚 **The Hand Game — Level 5: Closed Fist (Consolidate)**
> Same as Day 9 game. Add a small twist: after the dog disengages from the fist and you open your hand, pause one full second before letting them take it. You're introducing a micro-delay between behavior and reward. This is marker-timing prep. Keep reps clean, 10–15 total.

---

## Phase 2 — Markers, Engagement, and Three Base Positions (Days 11–20)

---

### Day 11
**Title:** Pick Your Markers and Load the Clicker  
**Objective:** Pair the click with food using clean mechanical sequence.  
**Task:** Choose your marker (clicker or verbal "yes"). Two sessions today. Each rep: click → one-one-thousand → reach → pay. Food must NOT be pre-loaded in your hand before the click. Click and reach must be separate. Run 20–30 reps per session.  
**Gate:** Two sessions completed with clean click → pause → reach → pay sequence. No pre-loading, no simultaneous reach. Yes = on track.  
**Game:** 🎯 **The Click Game — Level 1: Load**
> This IS the training session for Day 11. The click game and the daily task are the same thing today. Your only score: did you keep the mechanics clean? One point per rep where your hand didn't move until after the click. Track your clean rep count across both sessions. Goal: 40+ clean reps total.

---

### Day 12
**Title:** Marker Conditioning, Session Two  
**Objective:** Test whether the marker is loaded. Look for the "break their neck" response.  
**Task:** Two more sessions with the same click → pause → reach → pay sequence. At the end of the second session, run the marker test: dog relaxed in the room, click cold with no food visible — does the dog snap toward you expecting food?  
**Gate:** Dog passes the marker test (snaps toward you on cold click). Strong pass = advance. Slow/mild = repeat today. Failed = back to Day 11, clean up mechanics.  
**Game:** 🎯 **The Click Game — Level 2: Cold Test**
> At the end of your session, walk away from your food bowl. Let the dog settle. Click once out of nowhere. How fast do they come? Score it: 0–1 second = loaded. 1–3 seconds = almost there. 3+ seconds or no response = needs more reps. Log your score and repeat sessions until you hit sub-2 seconds consistently.

---

### Day 13
**Title:** Engagement — I Am the Button You Push  
**Objective:** Dog learns that orienting to the handler produces value. Handler is not the food, but the way to the food.  
**Task:** Two sessions. Closed-fist drill: present fist, wait for dog to disengage and look at your face, click the moment their eyes come up, pay. Don't reward nosing or pawing at the fist — only the re-orient to your face earns the click.  
**Gate:** By end of two sessions, dog offers a clear re-orient (face check) within 5 seconds of fist presentation. Yes = advance to Day 15. Inconsistent = repeat today.  
**Game:** 🎯 **The Click Game — Level 3: Eye Contact**
> After the fist drill warmup, set the fist aside. Stand in front of your dog. Wait. The moment they make deliberate eye contact with you — click and pay. That's it. No prompt, no cue, no lure. You're shaping eye contact as a default behavior. Run 10–15 reps. Dog that offers eye contact quickly has an engagement baseline. Dog that wanders = needs more closed-fist work first.

---

### Day 14
**Title:** The Hand-in-Face Engagement Drill  
**Objective:** Increase engagement intensity. Dog learns that persistence doesn't pay — attention does.  
**Task:** Same as Day 13 but add distraction: while dog is eating from your hand, slowly bring your other hand close to their face (not touching). Wait for them to ignore the hand and look up at your eyes. Click and pay the eye contact. Build duration before the click.  
**Gate:** Dog reliably re-orients to your face when a hand is presented near their face, without chasing the hand. Consistent = advance. Still hand-fixated = repeat.  
**Game:** 🎯 **The Click Game — Level 3: Eye Contact (Duration)**
> Same as Day 13 game but add 1 second of duration. After the dog makes eye contact, hold it for one full second before clicking. If they look away before the second is up, no click — just reset and try again. Gradually build from 1 second to 3 seconds over the session. Don't rush it.

---

### Day 15
**Title:** Free-Shape Session 1 — The First Base Position  
**Objective:** Introduce free-shaping. Dog offers a sit (or down) without being lured.  
**Task:** Dog on loose leash underfoot, quiet room. Stand still. Click and pay any movement toward sitting. Let the dog figure it out. You are not prompting, luring, or naming anything yet. Just reward successive approximations toward the target position.  
**Gate:** Dog offered the target position and got paid for it multiple times in today's sessions. Yes = advance. Dog wandered without offering anything = food drive may be under where it needs to be. Check hunger level.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 1: Sit**
> Your scoring metric: how many unprompted sits did you click in each 5-minute session? Target is 10+ in a session by end of day. If you're below 5, your dog is either not hungry enough or over-stimulated by the room. Adjust environment first, hunger second.

---

### Day 16
**Title:** Free-Shape — Second Session, Same Position  
**Objective:** Volume of clean reps locks the behavior in. Build fluency.  
**Task:** Same setup, same position target. Your click criteria tightens today — only crisp, clean reps earn the click. If the dog adds behaviors (pawing, spinning, jumping) do not click. Wait it out.  
**Gate:** Dog offering the position consistently and quickly across both sessions. Clicking only clean reps. Yes = advance. Position not reliable = stay here.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 1: Sit (Fluency)**
> Track the time between when you reset and when your dog offers the sit. Your target: under 3 seconds. If they're sitting fast and clean, you're ready to move on. If they're experimenting with other behaviors (down, paw, spin), stay here and only click the sit.

---

### Day 17
**Title:** Free-Shape the Second Position  
**Objective:** Introduce the second base position (if sit was first, now shape down; if down was first, shape sit).  
**Task:** Same setup but a different target position. The dog may offer the first position out of habit — don't click it. Wait for any movement toward the new position and click that. This will feel messy at first.  
**Gate:** Dog offered the second position multiple times. Starting to favor the new position over the old one. Yes = advance. Position not established = repeat.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 2: Down**
> If you were shaping sit, now you want any weight shift downward. Click for elbows moving toward the floor — you don't need a full down yet. Track how many downward movements you clicked in the session. Progression: weight shift → elbows lower → full down. Don't rush the final position.

---

### Day 18
**Title:** Free-Shape Place  
**Objective:** Shape the place behavior. Dog learns to get fully onto the raised cot.  
**Task:** Put the raised cot in the room. Let the dog investigate it. Click any interaction with it: sniffing it, touching it, stepping toward it. Gradually raise your criteria toward full four-on placement. This is a chained behavior — it will take longer than sit or down.  
**Gate:** Dog is at minimum getting onto the place bed by end of today's second session. Full four-on = great. Stepping onto it = fine, keep going. Just looking at it = one more day before advancing.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 3: Place**
> Criteria ladder: (1) dog looks at the cot → click; (2) dog steps toward it → click; (3) front paws on → click; (4) all four paws on → jackpot. Work through the ladder in one session. Note which step you're on at the end of the session. That's your starting point tomorrow.

---

### Day 19
**Title:** Cleaning Up — All Three Positions, All Reliably Offered  
**Objective:** Add duration to place. Clean up sit and down switching.  
**Task:** Two parts. Part 1: Mixed session of sit and down, switching between them cleanly. Part 2: Place with duration — click only after dog has held the position for 15–30 seconds. Build the hold before the click.  
**Gate:** Dog holds place 15–30 seconds before the click. Switching between sit and down comfortably in mixed sessions. Yes to both = advance. No to either = stay here.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 3: Place (Duration)**
> After the dog loads onto the place bed, start counting seconds before you click. Begin at 5 seconds and add 5 seconds per successful rep, up to 30. If the dog breaks early, no click — calmly redirect back and start the count over. Don't use a verbal "stay" yet. Duration is coming from the withholding of the click, not from a command.

---

### Day 20
**Title:** Week 2 Checkpoint  
**Objective:** Confirm all three positions are load-bearing before adding cues and pressure.  
**Task:** Five-question assessment:
1. Dog offers sit unprompted in the training setup?
2. Dog offers down unprompted?
3. Dog gets on place cot unprompted and holds for 15+ seconds?
4. Dog switches between sit and down in a mixed session?
5. Marker is still loading clean (cold click produces orientation)?  
**Gate:** All five yes = advance to Day 21. Any no = identify the gap and address before moving on.  
**Game:** 🔲 **The Shape Game — Level 4: Mix**
> Run a 10-minute session switching between all three positions — sit, down, place. No cues. You're clicking and paying the offers as they come. Track how many times you clicked each one. A balanced mix (all three appearing without you prompting) means the behaviors are solid. If one behavior dominates, you need more reps on the others before advancing.

## Phase 3 — Cues, Pressure, and Indoor Distraction Proofing (Days 21–30)

---

### Day 21
**Title:** Add the Verbal Cue to the First Position  
**Objective:** Transfer the first behavior onto a verbal cue using the 1.4-second rule.  
**Task:** Wait for the dog to be in the process of offering the first position. Just before they hit the position, say the cue word one time. Click when they hit the position, pay. The cue goes BEFORE the behavior, not after. Repeat 20–30 reps across two sessions.  
**Gate:** By end of today, can you say the cue and have the dog drop into the position most of the time even when they weren't about to offer it? Yes = advance. No = more reps, tighten timing.  
**Game:** 🗣️ **The Cue Game — Level 1: Name It**
> Test: stand in front of your dog. Say the cue once. Does the dog respond within 2 seconds? Score: yes within 2 seconds = loaded. Yes but slow (3–4 seconds) = almost there, more reps. No response = cue is not loaded yet. Log your response rate across 10 trials. Target: 8 out of 10.

---

### Day 22
**Title:** Add the Verbal Cues to the Other Two Positions  
**Objective:** Load cues onto the second and third base behaviors. Three commands loaded by end of day.  
**Task:** Same timing drill as Day 21, applied to the remaining two positions. Run each in separate sessions to avoid confusion. End the day by doing a mixed cue session — say each cue once and see if the dog responds correctly.  
**Gate:** Three loaded verbal cues that produce the corresponding behaviors most of the time. Yes = advance. Weak cue = run another single-cue session on it before advancing.  
**Game:** 🗣️ **The Cue Game — Level 2: Three Commands**
> Give each cue once in random order across 15 trials (5 per command, randomized). Score each response: correct within 2 seconds = 1 point. Slow or wrong = 0 points. Target: 12/15 or better. Below 10/15 = identify which cue is weak and drill it separately.

---

### Day 23
**Title:** Pressure Conditioning, Day One  
**Objective:** Introduce leash pressure as a language, not a restraint.  
**Task:** Light leash pressure sideways or forward. The moment the dog steps toward you to release the pressure, click and pay. You are conditioning: pressure = move toward paycheck. Run 50–100 reps. Keep pressure light. No corrections, no frustration. Clean mechanics only.  
**Gate:** 50–100 clean reps with light pressure → dog moving forward → leash goes slack → food delivered. Yes = advance. Too heavy or reps incomplete = repeat.  
**Game:** 📿 **The Leash Game — Level 1: Follow the Pressure**
> This IS today's conditioning session. Your score: how many reps did you run where the pressure was light enough that the dog chose to move toward it rather than fight it? A dog pulling against pressure doesn't count. A dog flowing toward you counts. Track your flow reps. Goal: 40+ flow reps in the session.

---

### Day 24
**Title:** Pressure Conditioning, Day Two  
**Objective:** Increase rep count and test whether the conditioning has taken hold.  
**Task:** Same drill as Day 23. More reps. At the end of the session, run the cold test: apply light pressure with no food visible. Does the dog snap toward you expecting food?  
**Gate:** Dog passes the pressure-conditioning test — snap response toward handler on cold pressure. Strong pass = advance. Slow look = advance with caution. Failed = reset Day 23.  
**Game:** 📿 **The Leash Game — Level 2: Cold Test**
> At the end of your session, put your food away. Let the dog settle. Apply light leash pressure once. How fast do they move toward you? Score: immediate (under 1 second) = conditioning is there. 1–3 seconds = close. 3+ seconds or opposition = needs more reps. Log your result.

---

### Day 25
**Title:** Marrying Pressure to a Known Cue  
**Objective:** Connect leash pressure to the first verbal cue. Dual-cue understanding: audible + tactile.  
**Task:** Say the verbal cue. If no response within 1.5 seconds, apply light pressure. The moment the dog moves into the position (from either the cue alone or the pressure assist), click and pay. 15–20 clean reps.  
**Gate:** 15–20 reps of position-with-pressure, no fighting, clean mark and pay, pressure releases on completion. Yes = advance. Any issues = identify (pressure too heavy? release too late? cue confusion?) and fix before moving on.  
**Game:** 📿 **The Leash Game — Level 3: Cue + Pressure**
> Run 10 trials. Give the cue. If dog responds within 1.5 seconds: click and pay, no pressure needed. If they don't: pressure assist → dog moves into position → click and pay. Track how many trials needed pressure vs. responded to the verbal alone. Target by end of session: 6+ out of 10 responding to verbal only.

---

### Day 26
**Title:** Marrying Pressure to the Other Two Positions  
**Objective:** All three base behaviors now have both verbal cue and pressure cue loaded.  
**Task:** Same dual-cue protocol from Day 25, applied to the remaining two positions. Run each in a separate session. End with a short mixed session across all three.  
**Gate:** Clean reps of both remaining positions with pressure, dog not fighting, pressure releasing cleanly on completion. Yes = advance. Weak position = re-run that session before advancing.  
**Game:** 📿 **The Leash Game — Level 4: Three Commands Under Pressure**
> Mixed session: 15 trials across all three commands, randomized. Each trial: give verbal cue → if no response, pressure assist → click and pay completion. Score: how many responded to verbal alone (no assist needed)? Target: 10+ out of 15 verbal-only responses.

---

### Day 27
**Title:** Indoor Distraction Proofing, Layer 1 — Handler Movement  
**Objective:** Dog holds position while the handler moves around them.  
**Task:** Dog in position. Handler walks away, turns around, reaches down, steps side to side — progressively increasing movement. Click and pay duration in position. If dog breaks, calmly re-cue and restart. Build through distraction levels 1–5.  
**Gate:** Dog holds each of the three positions through at least handler distraction levels 1–3. Levels 4–5 is the target. Not there yet = more reps at lower levels.  
**Game:** 🏋️ **The Hold Game — Level 1: Handler Movement**
> Distraction ladder: (1) you take one step back → dog holds; (2) two steps → holds; (3) turn around → holds; (4) crouch down → holds; (5) step out of the room briefly → holds. Track the highest level your dog held in today's session for each of the three positions. That's your starting point for tomorrow.

---

### Day 28
**Title:** Indoor Distraction Proofing, Layer 2 — Environmental  
**Objective:** Dog holds position while real-world environmental distractions occur.  
**Task:** Dog in position. Introduce: food on the floor at increasing distances, a crinkled paper ball rolled past, kitchen sounds, door knocking. Build in levels. Click and pay clean holds. If dog breaks, no punishment — re-cue and reduce the distraction level.  
**Gate:** Dog holds through food on the floor at multiple distances and some sound-based distractions. Yes = advance. No = stay here with more reps at lower levels.  
**Game:** 🏋️ **The Hold Game — Level 2: Environmental**
> Distraction ladder: (1) food on floor 10 feet away → dog holds; (2) food 5 feet away → holds; (3) food 2 feet away → holds; (4) food slides past on the floor → holds; (5) loud noise (knock, drop something) → holds. Track highest level held per position. Goal: level 3+ across all three positions.

---

### Day 29
**Title:** Indoor Distraction Proofing, Layer 3 — Social  
**Objective:** Dog holds position while other people move in the space.  
**Task:** A second household member enters the room, moves around, talks, ignores the dog. Dog must hold position. Build up to: doorbell, voices from another room, person crouching near the dog, person making brief eye contact with the dog.  
**Gate:** Dog holds through household-member movement (levels 1–4) and some social-sound distractions (levels 5–6). Yes = advance to Day 30. No = stay here with more reps at lower social levels.  
**Game:** 🏋️ **The Hold Game — Level 3: Social**
> Social distraction ladder: (1) second person walks through the room → holds; (2) person sits nearby → holds; (3) person talks to the handler → holds; (4) person crouches near dog → holds; (5) doorbell sound → holds; (6) person briefly makes eye contact with dog → holds. Track highest level held. Goal: level 4+ by end of session.

---

### Day 30
**Title:** Final Indoor Diagnostic + What Comes Next  
**Objective:** Confirm the dog has working indoor obedience against real distractions. Establish baseline for Phase 2 of the program.  
**Task:** Run the full diagnostic. Give each of the three verbal cues in a real-life environment (not the training setup). Introduce a real environmental distraction and ask for a hold. Use pressure as a backup tool and see if it's still clean. Evaluate what's solid vs. what needs continued work before taking things outside.  
**Gate:** No formal gate — this is a diagnostic. Document where the dog lands on each dimension. Honest self-assessment of what's ready to take into outdoor work vs. what needs more indoor time.  
**Game:** 🏆 **The Full Run — Day 30 Diagnostic**
> Run all three commands in three different locations inside the house. Run the hold with one real distraction per position. Score each: solid (responds and holds without pressure) / conditional (responds but needs backup) / not yet (breaks or doesn't respond). This is your program completion snapshot. Log it. It becomes your Day 1 baseline for the next phase.

## App Implementation Notes

- **Phase 1 (Days 1–10):** No formal game UI needed yet. Hand Game levels are tracked by handler self-report (number of reps, engagement quality). Simple input fields.
- **Phase 2 (Days 11–20):** Click/Shape games can use a rep counter. Handler logs clean reps vs. sloppy reps. App shows daily rep target.
- **Phase 3 (Days 21–30):** Cue and Hold games use pass/partial/fail scoring per distraction level. App can display the distraction ladder as a checklist.
- **Gates:** Hard-coded. Each day's lesson is locked until the handler submits their checkpoint and it meets the gate criteria. No skipping.
- **Repeat logic:** If a gate fails, the app re-serves the same day. Streak tracking resets on food-drive protocol failures (Days 2–5).
